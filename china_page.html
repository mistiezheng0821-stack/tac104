China

Summer 2024

June 21, 2024 to August 23, 2025

About This Trip

  • When: June 21 to July 23, 2024
  • Who: Me, My family, and my favorite cousin
  • Why: To visit my grandparents and my parent's hometown
  • What: Stayed at our apartment, shopped, visited different provinces, and met up with different cousins.
  • Where: Fujian, Shanghai, and Zhejiang

Provinces I Went to & Things I Did!

Fujian

Fujian is my parents' hometown and where my grandparents live, so we spent most of our time there. On the first day we landed, we visited our grandma. She bought us traditional Chinese food that we can't find anywhere else. The next day, we got to our apartment, which is where I spent most of my free time. In this province, I did a lot of shopping since there was a shopping plaza right across from our apartment. We also visited the night markets oftenâ€”they always had really good food at extremely cheap prices, which I can't find in America. In Fujian, I also visited "Shangfang Qixiang," otherwise known as Three Lanes Seven Alleys. It was a street full of stores, vendors, shops, and food. I also went hiking to the mountains one day to see a temple that my dad had donated to. The view was beautiful. One of my favorite things about Fujian was the extremely cheap massages and head spas. My mom would take me almost every other day to get my hair washed by someone. Fujian is less developed, so I got to experience local transportation by waving for rides on bikes, which felt nostalgic. All my cousins from both sides of the family also came, so we spent time together. Overall, I mainly spent my time with family, tried traditional food and night markets, and did some light shopping.

Shanghai

The next province we visited was Shanghai, one of the most popular cities in China. This was a very different scenery from Fujian, as it is a lot more crowded and city-like. I took the bullet train with my family to get here. It was extremely hot, however I did most of my more fun activities here. One of the days here, we dedicated to shopping at this busy street. The Gentle Monster pop up collab with Jennie was hosted here, so I went inside to look around and take pictures. Most of the Jennie collab sunglasses were sold out, but I did end up buying a pair of glasses outside of the collab. After that, I visited the Muji store as it is MUCH cheaper here than in America. Lastly, I got to visit a cat cafe! It was really cute. We got to play with a bunch of cats for an hour. Then, the next day, we also visited the more tourist-y attractions. We visited The Bund; it was extremely beautiful at night but also extremely crowded. I loved seeing the night lights and how all the buildings lit up. It was truly beautiful in Shanghai. For my time here, I mainly spent my time shopping, taking pictures, and visiting modern cities.

Zhejiang

The last province I went to was Zhejiang! I would say this province was a bit between Fujian and Shanghai in terms of scenery. Zhejiang had a lot more of a naturalistic scenery compared to the city-like Shanghai. I met up with another one of my relatives here since he lived in Shanghai, and he lead us through all the attractions. It was amazing since he was local, so he knew all the less popular hidden spots. It was refreshing compared to the very busy Shanghai. The first thing we did was visit the Umbrella and Knives Museum. It was really interesting to see the the evolution of these everyday items. Next, we also visited a temple. At this temple, I also got to be dressed in a traditional Chinese hanfu. I ended off this province's trip going to a live concert that showcased performaers singing, dancing, and playing musical instruments on water. For my time in Zhejiang, I mainly spent time looking at its beautiful scenery and culture.

Reflections & Thoughts

I had a lot of fun in China! What I loved most about being here is getting to revisit my childhood and seeing all the traditional food and spots that I had when I was younger. It was also really nice to visit family, and overall the trip meant most to be because of the sentimental experiences I had. I loved the night markets, I felt like I really got to indulge with my culture. I also loved experiencing all the other activities that I can't get through America; for example the live concert concert on water. What I didn't like on the trip and think can be improved is definitely the weather. It was insanely hot, which really made me not want to go out despite all the time I had here. I also think I should've planned a better itinerary for local places I want to try so I can get the most out of my trip. I had a great time overall.

8/10
