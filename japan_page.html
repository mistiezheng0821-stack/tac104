Japan

Summer 2023

From June 21, 2024 to August 23, 2025

About This Trip

  • When: July 25 to August 1, 2023
  • Who: With my dance team and family
  • Why: For a dance competition
  • What: Competed at the Olympics stadium, explored Tokyo and Shinjuku with friends, spent time with family
  • Where: Tokyo

7-Day Itinerary

Day 1

Today was mostly spent traveling. After landing at 3pm, my dance team and I took a bus to the hotel, checked in, and unpacked. We made a few nearby 7/11 runs, and later I met up with my family for dinner.

Day 2

Today my dance team had a group outing. We wore matching shirts and toured Tokyo, stopping at Kokyo Gaien Garden, Senso-ji Temple, and Tokyo Tower in Shibakoen. After the tour, I had dinner with my family and stayed at their hotel.

Day 3

Most of today was spent with family at the Studio Ghibli Museum. After the museum, we shopped around Shibuya, then got dinner at the Tsukiji Fish Market. At night, I went back to the hotel with my dance team.

Day 4

Today was our team rehearsal day. We practiced from 10-2pm at a small studio. After rehearsal, I got Kura Sushi with my friends, then we shopped around Takeshita Street again. We ended off the night going to a pig cafe, grabbed dinner, then went back home.

Day 5

Today is competition Day 1! The whole team got to Tokyo Olympic Stadium at around 10am. We did our makeup, ran some pieces, the competed. After, we went back to our hotels and I got food from 711 with my friends.

Day 6

Today is competition Day 2! Today was very similar to yesterday. We spent most of our day at the competition. After competition, I got kebab with my friends for dinner.

Day 7

Today is our last day in Japan. Everyone on the team got a ticket to Tokyo's Disney, so we spent most of our day there. My family also went as well, so I was with both my friends and family. After Disney, we grabbed food and packed to go home tomorrow.

Day 8

Going back home today! I made one last stop at 711 to get their cheap and amazing food. I flew with my team back to Los Angeles.

Reflections & Thoughts

This trip meant so much to me because it's truly a once in a lifetime experience. I can always go back to Japan, but I'll never have to chance to be here with my entire dance team while competing at the Olympics Stadium. Because of that, I think Japan is my favorite travel so far. What I loved most about my trip was just getting to spend time with my friends. It was the first time out the country without my parents, so getting that new experience of going out at night at 2am with my friends for a quick stop at 711 felt so independent and freing despite it being such a "small" moment. I always loved competing; the competitions in Japan are not the same in the US. They have a whole celebration before they announce awards, which is where I got to interact with many different teams despite not knowing each other and the language barrier. It truly felt like a community. What I think can be improved, is once again the weather. I don't think I've sweated more in my life. I also did not plan a lot of the places that I wanted to go, so I think I missed out on a lot of potential restaurants and activities I could've tried my time there. Overall, I think this is such a significant travel in my life and will always be one of my favorite moments.

9/10
